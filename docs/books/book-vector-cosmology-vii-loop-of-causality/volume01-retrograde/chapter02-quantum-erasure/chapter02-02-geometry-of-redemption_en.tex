\subsubsection{The Orthogonality of Fact and Meaning}

First, we need to geometrically distinguish two concepts: \textbf{Fact ($v_{ext}$)} and \textbf{Meaning ($v_{int}$)}.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Fact}: Events occurring in the \textbf{external sector} of the great circle. For example, ``You fell in 2025.'' This is an objective history recorded in photons and atoms. According to the second law of thermodynamics, the entropy increase of this event is irreversible. You cannot reverse time to prevent the fall.

\item \textbf{Meaning}: The projection this event leaves in your \textbf{internal sector}. For example, ``Falling means I am a failure'' or ``Falling means I learned to watch the road.'' This is a \textbf{quantum state}.
\end{itemize}

The key point: \textbf{$v_{int}$ and $v_{ext}$ are orthogonal.}

This means that the same external fact can correspond to countless orthogonal internal meaning bases.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Trauma State}: Your consciousness is locked on the ``victim'' basis. Past events and your future form \textbf{Destructive Interference}. Every step forward, past memories produce a reverse phase, canceling your kinetic energy.

\item \textbf{Redemption State}: You rotate the observation basis. You redefine the same event as ``tempering.'' Now, past events and your future form \textbf{Constructive Interference}. Past pain transforms into \textbf{phase potential energy} pushing you upward.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Conclusion:}

We cannot change \textbf{``what happened''}.

But we have absolute power to define \textbf{``what it is''}.
