We can model a family or social unit as a \textbf{thermodynamic system}.

\begin{enumerate}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Role}: \textbf{Negentropy Generator}.
\item \textbf{Task}: Extract resources (negentropy) from the environment through high-intensity computation (work, decision-making), and block external disorder shocks.
\item \textbf{Physical state}: High $v_{int}$ (extremely fast internal computation), high free energy gradient (high pressure), high-frequency oscillation. This is the ``crazy working'' you.
\end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Rear (The Rear)}: The internal region protected by the frontline.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Role}: \textbf{Order Maintainer}.
\item \textbf{Task}: Use the negentropy delivered from the frontline to perform low-frequency, fine structural repairs (sleep, emotional exchange, child-rearing), maintaining the system's long-term stability.
\item \textbf{Physical state}: Low $v_{int}$ (slow explicit computation), low free energy (stable), long-range coherence. This is the ``peacefully sleeping'' family.
\end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

If everyone sleeps peacefully in the rear, the system will starve due to lack of negentropy input (heat death).
